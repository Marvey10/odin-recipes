Goulash

Description

Goulash is a soup or stew of meat and vegetables seasoned with paprika and other spices. Originating in Hungary, goulash is a common meal predominantly eaten in Central Europe but also in other parts of Europe. It is one of the national dishes of Hungary and a symbol of the country.

Ingredients

  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 3 onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons Hungarian sweet paprika
  • 3 teaspoons salt, divided
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Cook and stir onions in oil until soft, 2 to 4 minutes.
  3. Remove onions and set them aside.
  4. Combine paprika, 2 teaspoons salt, and pepper in a medium bowl.
  5. Coat beef cubes in spice mixture, and cook in the onion pot until brown on all sides.
  6. Return onions to the pot with beef; pour in water, tomato paste, garlic, and remaining 1 teaspoon salt.
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  8. Cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, until meat is tender, 1 1/2 to 2 hours.
